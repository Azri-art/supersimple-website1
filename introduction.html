Introduction

On the island of Borneo, in the Malaysian state of Sarawak, sits the Lundu District. Situated west of Kuching, the state capital, it is a part of the Kuching Division. Lundu is a well-liked travel destination for those who enjoy the outdoors and the environment because of its stunning natural surroundings, which include verdant beaches, calm rivers, and beautiful jungles.

Like a large portion of Borneo, Lundu has been inhabited for countless years. The Bidayuh, Iban, and other Dayak communities were probably among the first native inhabitants. These tribes created complex social structures and cultural customs while engaging in subsistence farming, hunting, and fishing.

In recent decades, Lundu has seen growth in tourism, driven by its natural attractions such as Gunung Gading National Park and its beautiful beaches. The district has also benefited from agricultural development, particularly in pepper and palm oil cultivation. Efforts have been made to preserve the cultural heritage of its indigenous communities while promoting sustainable development.
